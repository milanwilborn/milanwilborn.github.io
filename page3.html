Different materials have higher strengths in different directions, and perform better under different forces. 

Think about a dirt clod, those giant lumps of sand you can find in the garden. Ball it up in your hands and squeeze. Did it break? Nope! If anything, you've made it even stronger. Now grab an end of that dirt clod and pull. See how easy it was to break it this way? Materials all have different strengths depending on if they are being pushed or pulled on. There are many different types of strength: compressive (push), tensile (pull), flexural (bend) and more!

Similar to that dirt clod, concrete is one material that is strong in compression, but much weaker in tension. It is also one of the most common materials used in buildings (especially the really tall ones). In order to counter concrete's low tensile strength, engineers use reinforcement. Reinforcing concrete means adding rods made out of a material that does well under pulling forces within the concrete while it is still pourable. Typically, this is done using steel bars. Look back at the section on bamboo strength and you can recall that bamboo can reach a tensile strength equal to or even greater than steel! This is what makes bamboo such a reasonable and innovative alternative in building designs.
