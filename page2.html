Sustainability

Bamboo offers a more environmentally friendly way of building materials

 

develop own website
Producing steel uses a lot of energy, labor, and of course  - money! This intense process leads to large amounts of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases emitted into the atmosphere. The harvesting of bamboo, however, is a much more natural process and comes with much lower environmental impacts. 
Along with the greener production methods, bamboo in place of steel provides the opportunity for less wealthy nations to build cities using less expensive resources!

Bamboo Production Facts

   

  • Bamboo can be harvested within 3 to 5 years after planting. This may seem like a long time, but compare it to the 10 years it takes to harvest wood from trees!
  • A study done by researchers in Switzerland found that bamboo reinforced concrete leads to overall environmental savings of about 48% compared to traditional steel reinforced concrete
  • From the same study, bamboo reinforcement was found to have a lower environmental impact than steel reinforcement in regards to climate change, resources, quality of ecosystem, and human health

Potential for Global Development

Beyond being better for the environment, using bamboo as a building material could also drastically change how cities are built all over the world!

Because bamboo is so commonly found in countries all over the world, less wealthy nations can save on the costs associated with shipping materials. Also, recall that the process of forming steel is very energy intensive. This is because it involves melting metals into a liquid form. Imagine how much heat you would have to apply to turn a quarter! Imagine, but don't try it!
Producing large amounts of energy requires a lot of labor, time, and therefore, money. Because of this, using steel in buildings often puts a strain on the development of cities in less wealthy nations. Bamboo presents itself as a much cheaper alternative, allowing nations to urbanize in ways that no one else could have in the past! Another benefit is that the bamboo oftentimes grows local to so many regions. All of the harvesting, processing, transport, and construction boosts employment and opens the door for a healthier, stronger economy!
