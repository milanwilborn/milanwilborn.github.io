Imagine a rubber band being stretched and stretched until it finally snaps. How hard did you pull it before it broke? More importantly, how far were you able to stretch it? Tensile stength describes how much a material can be pulled before it breaks. Bamboo can be manipulated to have a tensile strength equal or greater to that of steel!  
Bamboo taken straight out of the ground (raw bamboo) can reach a tensile strength around 370 MPa. This is equal to the tensile strength of low carbon steel. With some additional processing of the bamboo, this strength can be made even greater! So how exactly does a bamboo plant reach such high strengths? It's all about the composition. 

Bamboo's super strength starts with how it is composed

When the wind blows, bamboo bends as anything planted in the earth would. Where some other plants may break due to this wind force, bamboo counters this force by concentrating its inner material to be more densely packed along the edge that bends.
